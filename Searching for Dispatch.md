# Nahk

## The Scene

The fire burns low.

One by one...

The sounds of insects disappear.

The wind stops.

The fire is still burning...

but no longer gives off heat.

One player notices someone sitting on a fallen log across the fire.

Not appearing.

Not materializing.

It's as if he'd simply been there the whole time.

Nahk.

He's wearing ordinary pre-war clothing under a weathered coat.

No weapon.

No armor.

He smiles.

Not sinister.

Just...

Friendly.

---

He breaks the silence.

> "Evening."

He looks around the fire.

> "Mind if I sit here a minute?"

He chuckles softly.

> "...I suppose I already am."

He gestures toward the coffee pot hanging over the fire.

> "Smells better than what my people make."

---

No threats.

No speeches.

Just conversation.

If someone draws a weapon...

He glances at it.

> "Go ahead if it makes you feel better."

Then back to the fire.

---

If someone asks,

> "Are you real?"

He smiles.

> "Depends."

> "You're dreaming."

> "I'm thinking."

> "Somewhere in the middle we happen to be talking."

---

If they ask how he's doing this.

He shrugs.

> "Wish I knew."

> "One day I woke up hearing people."

> "Then eventually..."

> "...they started hearing me."

---

Then he changes the subject.

> "You did well in Vault 87."

A pause.

> "The Prototype was suffering."

> "You did him a kindness."

That immediately tells the players something unexpected:

Nahk didn't see the Prototype as a weapon.

He saw him as a tragedy.

---

He leans back.

> "I'm sorry about sending you there."

Pause.

> "...well..."

He smiles.

> "Not really."

> "I needed to know what kind of people you were."

---

If someone asks,

> "So this was a test?"

He nods.

> "Everything's a test."

> "For me."

> "For you."

> "For the wasteland."

---

> "You're going to the Citadel."

Players:

> "How do you know?"

He smiles.

> "Because I'd go to the Citadel."

Then...

> "They're good people."

> "Most of them."

> "Just remember..."

He pokes the fire with a stick.

> "Organizations don't make decisions."

> "People do."

Pause.

> "People get scared."

Then...

> "When frightened people gain power..."

He looks up.

> "History gets repetitive."
---

Then he asks them a question.

Not rhetorical.

A genuine one.

> "Tell me..."

> "How many people have you saved?"

Let them answer.

No matter what they say...

He nods.

> "Good."

Then...

> "How many died because you couldn't get there in time?"

Silence.

---

He isn't accusing them.

He's genuinely asking.

---

Then...

> "Do you know how many voices I hear every day?"

He stares into the fire.

> "Hungry."

> "Afraid."

> "Dying."

> "Children."

> "Old men."

> "Raiders."

> "Mutants."

> "Doesn't matter."

> "They all sound the same when they're scared."

---

He finally looks back up.

> "I got tired of listening."

---

That's his motivation.

Not conquest.

Not revenge.

He believes civilization has failed.

---

Then...

> "You know what everyone keeps trying?"

He starts counting on his fingers.

> "Governments."

> "Kings."

> "Presidents."

> "Councils."

> "Elders."

> "Every single one says..."

He smiles.

> "'This time it'll work.'"

---

Then...

> "It never does."

---

If someone says,

> "You're raising an army."

He nods immediately.

> "I am."

No denial.

---

> "Because armies are the only language history has ever respected."

---

Then...

> "The difference is..."

He smiles again.

> "...mine already agrees."

---


He stares at the embers for a time

---


> "You know..."

> "I'd rather you didn't die."

One player asks why.

He genuinely looks confused.

> "Because you're useful."

A beat.

Then he laughs.

> "That came out wrong."

---

He thinks.

Then says...

> "Because I like you."

Complete honesty.

---

> "Most people stop asking questions."

> "You keep asking."

> "That's rare."

---

Finally...

He stands.

Brushes dust off his coat.

> "I'm not asking you to trust me."

> "Just..."

He points toward the horizon.

> "...keep looking."

> "Eventually you'll see what I saw."

---

Then one last smile.

> "And if you don't..."

A shrug.

> "We'll probably end up trying to kill each other."

No anger.

Just inevitability.

---

He starts walking away into the darkness.

One player calls after him.

> "Where are you?"

Without turning around...

> "Closer than the Brotherhood."

Pause.

> "...farther away than you'd believe."

Then the dream simply ends.

# Old Olney: The Hunting Ground

## Core premise

Old Olney contains **Presidential Relay Site 01**, hidden beneath a pre-war telecommunications exchange.

Unfortunately, the exchange now stands at the center of a large Deathclaw nesting territory.

The Deathclaws are not randomly wandering through town. They have established:

- Hunting routes
- Nesting chambers
- Feeding grounds
- Ambush positions
- A dominant breeding pair
- Juveniles protected deeper within the territory

The party is entering an active ecosystem.

Their objective remains:

> Reach the relay site, recover its BLACKLINE records, and escape.

They are not required to kill every Deathclaw. However, they can attempt to break the nest if they believe they are capable.

---

# Establishing the threat

As they approach Old Olney, the first thing they find is a battlefield.

Oddly all of the snow is melted here around the perimeter of the city.

A Brotherhood patrol attempted to enter several years earlier.

The wreckage includes:

- A torn-open armored personnel carrier
- Deep claw furrows through its steel plating
- A Power Armor torso separated from both legs
- A Gatling laser with its barrel bent sideways
- Three distinct trails where bodies were dragged away
- Spent ammunition covering the roadway

The disturbing detail is that the Brotherhood did not appear to be ambushed.

They formed a defensive position.

They knew the Deathclaws were coming.

It did not matter.

A surviving field report can be recovered from the wreck:

> “Contact on all sides.”

> “They’re not charging the barricade.”

> “They’re circling us.”

> “Something larger is directing them.”

Then the recording ends.

That immediately tells the players this is not a normal collection of creatures.

---

# The Deathclaw pack

I would build the Old Olney nest around four combat roles.

## Deathclaw Stalkers

These are the first creatures the party encounters.

They stay at medium distance and probe the group.

They:

- Move between buildings
- Attack isolated characters
- Withdraw after taking damage
- Attempt to draw players away from the group
- Climb onto roofs and collapsed structures

Their job is not necessarily to kill anyone.

Their job is to determine whether the party is prey.

---

## Deathclaw Hunters

These are the primary combatants.

They engage once the Stalkers have identified the party’s weak points.

They:

- Rush ranged attackers
- Attempt to knock characters prone
- Push targets behind cover or into buildings
- Focus on wounded opponents
- Use ruined streets to break line of sight

These should feel powerful, but manageable as a conventional combat encounter.

---

## The Broodmother

She remains near the central nest and Relay Site.

She is older, scarred, and significantly larger than the others.

She does not immediately attack.

She watches from the shadows while the pack fights.

If the players begin winning, enter the nest, or threaten the juveniles, she joins the battle.

Her arrival should change the fight completely.

Possible traits:

- A roar that grants nearby Deathclaws an immediate movement
- A tail sweep that strikes multiple nearby characters
- The ability to call another Deathclaw from a tunnel or building
- Increased resistance while protecting the nest
- A brutal reaction attack against anyone moving past her

She should feel like the commander referenced in the old Brotherhood recording.

---

## The Old Male

This is the true optional boss.

He is not always present.

He patrols the wider territory and may be drawn back by:

- Sustained gunfire
- Explosions
- The relay activating
- The Broodmother’s roar
- The death of several pack members

He is not larger merely for spectacle. He is ancient, heavily scarred, and has survived repeated attempts to clear Old Olney.

He should enter only if the party commits to a major battle or remains too long.

This creates escalation without forcing an unfair encounter at the beginning.

---

# The approach to the relay

The telecommunications exchange stands in the middle of a broad intersection.

That makes it intentionally difficult to approach.

Around it are:

- A collapsed parking garage
- An overturned bus
- Two partially intact office buildings
- A storm-drain entrance
- A shattered elevated roadway
- Several concealed Deathclaw tunnels

The party can observe the area before entering.

They may notice Deathclaws using regular routes:

- One crossing between the parking garage and the exchange
- One disappearing into the storm drain
- Another resting beneath the elevated roadway
- A juvenile briefly appearing near a hole in the exchange wall

This gives them meaningful options.

They can:

- Sneak through the storm drains
- Create a distraction elsewhere in town
- Establish a firing position
- Mine likely approach routes
- Collapse one of the tunnels
- Directly assault the exchange
- Attempt to lure individual Deathclaws away

---

# The first major combat

The initial serious encounter should happen outside the exchange.

A good composition would be:

- Two Stalkers
- One Hunter
- A second Hunter entering after one round or after loud gunfire

The Stalkers begin on rooftops or inside ruined buildings.

The Hunter attacks from street level.

The delayed creature arrives from a tunnel behind the party.

This creates an immediate tactical problem:

> The Deathclaws are attempting to divide the group.

The players can win this fight cleanly, but it should cost ammunition, Action Points, healing, or positioning.

Afterward, they hear a distant roar.

Not close.

Not yet.

That is the warning that the pack has noticed them.

---

# Inside the telecommunications exchange

The building should not be safe.

The upper floors contain evidence of the Enclave expedition:

- Portable work lights
- Empty ammunition containers
- Cut-open maintenance panels
- Two dead Enclave soldiers
- A barricaded stairwell
- A field terminal containing the reference to a signal below

The Enclave reached the building, but not the relay.

One of their final logs reads:

> “We were wrong about the ruins.”

> “The creatures are entering through the utility level.”

> “They know where we are.”

> “They are waiting for us to attempt evacuation.”

The basement stairs have collapsed.

The party must descend through the old telephone cable vaults.

---

# The underground nest

The utility tunnels beneath the exchange have become part of the Deathclaw nest.

This is where the scenario becomes menacing.

The tunnels contain:

- Bones packed into cable trenches
- Brotherhood dog tags
- Enclave helmets
- Half-eaten Yao Guai
- Egg chambers
- Deep scratches around the sealed relay door
- Several places where Deathclaws have attempted to dig through reinforced concrete

The Deathclaws know something exists beyond the door.

They simply cannot enter it.

The party must pass through or near the nesting chamber to reach the relay.

---

# The optional nest battle

If the players choose to attack rather than sneak around the nest, this should become the session’s major combat set piece.

## Battlefield

Use a large underground switching chamber containing:

- Raised maintenance platforms
- Cable trenches
- Hanging electrical lines
- Concrete support columns
- A damaged transformer
- Two tunnel entrances
- The sealed relay blast door
- A partially collapsed ceiling

This creates verticality, hazards, and defensible positions.

## Enemy escalation

Begin with:

- Two Hunters
- One Stalker

Then escalate based on player actions.

### Escalation 1

When the first Deathclaw is killed:

- The Broodmother enters through the nesting chamber.

### Escalation 2

If the party uses explosives or activates heavy machinery:

- Another Hunter enters from a side tunnel.

### Escalation 3

If the fight continues for several rounds or the Broodmother is killed:

- The Old Male arrives.

The Old Male should not simply appear beside them. His arrival should be telegraphed:

- Dust falls from the ceiling.
- A distant metal door buckles.
- Heavy footsteps approach.
- One surviving Deathclaw retreats instead of attacking.
- Then the wall or tunnel barricade gives way.

That gives the players one round to decide whether to hold, flee, or reach the relay.

---

# Environmental combat options

The players should be able to use the room against the Deathclaws.

## Live electrical cables

A successful Repair or Science action can energize exposed cables.

Possible result:

- Energy damage in a limited area
- Temporary stun
- Prevents use of one tunnel for a round

## Transformer overload

The party can overload the damaged transformer.

This creates:

- A powerful blast
- Temporary darkness
- Electrical fire
- Possible structural instability

It should be effective, but dangerous to everyone.

## Cable trenches

Characters can jump across them.

Deathclaws can also cross them, but a knocked-prone creature may fall inside and lose movement.

## Collapsible platform

The players can destroy a support to drop a platform onto a Deathclaw or block a route.

## Relay blast door

Once opened, the door can become the party’s final defensive position.

The players may retreat through it and attempt to close it while Deathclaws force their way inside.

That creates an excellent cinematic ending to the battle.

# The dangerous exit

Entering the relay does not automatically solve their escape.

They discover that the facility has a secondary emergency egress route.

Unfortunately, the map shows that it exits several blocks away through a utility structure.

They now choose:

- Return through the nest
- Use the unknown emergency tunnel
- Remain inside and attempt to seal or kill the Deathclaws outside
- Use the relay equipment to create a diversion elsewhere

This keeps the tension alive after the information is recovered.
