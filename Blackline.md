### Project BLACKLINE (working title)

During the Cold War, the federal government constructed a classified transportation network.

Not for commuters.

For continuity of government.

Think:

- The White House
- The Pentagon
- Raven Rock
- Mount Weather
- Secure laboratories
- Military command bunkers
- Federal Reserve
- Intelligence facilities

All connected.

No public maps.

No public entrances.

---

Now your radio tower makes perfect sense.

It wasn't powering trains.

It was restoring communications to:

- relay stations
- encrypted repeaters
- automated dispatch systems

That network has been asleep for 200 years.

Until now.

---

## Even better...

The Enclave didn't know everything.

I actually **wouldn't** make the Enclave fully aware of BLACKLINE.

That makes the world feel deeper.

Instead...

The Enclave inherited fragments.

Old references.

Incomplete records.

Enough for Nahk to piece together.

Imagine finding one sentence in an Enclave archive:

> BLACKLINE access requires Relay Synchronization.

No explanation.

Just that.

Nahk spends years figuring it out.

---

## How Nahk found it

This is where his intelligence shines.

Not because someone told him.

Because he solved it.

Maybe he spent years reading:

- Enclave databases
- military engineering manuals
- presidential archives
- satellite records

Everyone else was trying to kill people.

Nahk was researching.

Eventually...

He discovers BLACKLINE.

Not active.

Dead.

But intact.

---

Then...

He spends years rebuilding it.

Not all of it.

Just enough.

Now suddenly...

His army can move between distant parts of DC.

Explaining:

- Megaton
- Arefu
- disappearances
- troop movements

without marching across the surface.
