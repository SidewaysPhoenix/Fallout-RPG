# 📬 GM Handoff Note

*To be given to your GM privately after the session — not shown to other players.*

---

Hey —

The one-shot you just saw is a **self-contained gift** to your campaign. Nothing in it changes your world. Here's what it quietly adds, if you want it:

---

## Vault 123 & PROJECT HOLLOWAY — What It Is

Your world now contains **Vault 123**, somewhere in your geography.

Its experiment: **PROJECT HOLLOWAY — Personality Matrix Extraction & Archival.** Named after Dr. Lenora Holloway, a pre-war Vault-Tec psychologist who believed personality could only be fully revealed through high-stakes narrative immersion — never self-reported, never observed, only *lived.*

The process: subjects were sealed into stasis, then fed a fully constructed psychological scenario via neural interface. They experienced it as real. While they lived it, Vault-Tec's systems mapped, recorded, and archived their complete personality — every decision, every fear, every moral instinct.

When the scenario ended, the subjects stayed in stasis. They have no memory of the extraction. As far as they know, they simply went to sleep.

**The player characters are the originals.** Their personalities also exist somewhere else now, in archived form. What Vault-Tec intended to *do* with those archives is the question your campaign can answer — or leave unanswered indefinitely.

---

## What's Deliberately Left Open

These are yours to define or ignore entirely:

- **Where is Vault 123?** Anywhere that fits your map.
- **What is "matrix deployment"?** RoboBrains? Synth precursors? A Vault-Tec AI trained on human personalities? A pre-war weapon? You decide.
- **Who is Dr. Lenora Holloway?** Dead, probably. But her research files are somewhere.
- **Who is the Overseer, and why are they offline?**
- **Where are the archived matrices?** A server in the vault? An offsite Vault-Tec facility? Lost entirely?
- **Did the extraction bleed through?** Elias's journal has a fragment suggesting something crossed the barrier between the scenario and the subjects' real memories. How far does that go?
- **When do the characters wake up?** Your call. Maybe never. Maybe exactly when you need them.

---

## The Bleed-Through Detail

One small thing worth knowing: Elias's journal contains an entry — the first one — that reads like a memory of the real world leaking into the simulation. A woman in a white coat. Clean walls. A hum.

The players may not connect it on the night. But if they ever wake up and go looking for answers about what happened to them, that journal entry is a thread they could pull.

---

## How to Use It (Options)

1. **Ignore it entirely.** The one-shot stands alone. No obligation.
2. **Background flavor.** Vault 123 exists on your map, sealed and silent.
3. **Mystery hook.** Someone in the wasteland has found references to PROJECT HOLLOWAY — and is looking for the vault, or the matrices, or both.
4. **The characters wake up.** Confused, out of time, carrying memories of a swamp that was constructed specifically to reveal who they are. They may eventually ask: *what's running our copies?*
5. **Full arc.** The matrices were deployed. Something in the wasteland has their personalities. It may or may not know that.

---

## What the Players Know

They know their characters are in Vault 123, in stasis, after something called PROJECT HOLLOWAY. They know their personalities were extracted and archived. They don't know what for.

That curiosity is yours.
