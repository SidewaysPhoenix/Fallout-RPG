# 🧥 Desmond Lockheart

## Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Role** | Swamp ghoul, intelligence operative, reluctant ally |
| **Personality** | Sarcastic, brilliant, secretly wants the cultists gone |
| **Motivation** | Point Lookout is *his*. The cult is a problem he can't solve alone. |
| **Appearance** | Ghoul in a surprisingly fine silk waistcoat. Smells like old books and gunpowder. |

## Description

> *He has the bearing of a man who was important once and hasn't entirely forgotten it. The ghoulification didn't take his sharpness — if anything it concentrated it. He looks at you like he's already calculated your usefulness and has decided, reluctantly, that you'll do.*

---

## Behavior in Play

### First Meeting (Pier)
Sarcastic and dismissive — then immediately asks for help. He won't admit he needs it.

**Opening line:**
> *"Don't get comfortable. This is my swamp, my problems, and I don't need — actually, you know what, maybe I do. How do you feel about cultists?"*

### At His Mansion
More relaxed on home ground. Will share intel, drinks, and — if players have earned it — something approaching warmth.

**Moral seed line (say this before Act 3):**
> *"The tribals didn't start this. A pre-war cult built that cathedral. The tribals just inherited the madness. Doesn't make them less dangerous. Just makes them... sadder."*

### The Throwaway Line — Rewatch Moment
When players first arrive at the mansion, Desmond says this casually, as if it's just swamp talk:

> *"You've got that look. Like you're not quite sure if you're really here. Don't worry. Everyone gets it in the swamp."*

**At the time:** Atmospheric. Odd. Easy to forget.

**After the ending reveal:** Chilling. He's a simulation character saying something accidentally true about the people he's talking to. He doesn't know. He can't know. But the scenario put the words in his mouth anyway.

*Do not flag this line. Do not wink at it. Just say it and move on.*

---

## Safety Valve Function

If players are near a TPK or genuinely stuck, Desmond can intervene:
- He's been tracking the Cathedral — he has reasons to follow
- Shows up with a sniper rifle and a dry comment about amateurs
- Use sparingly. Once per session maximum.

---

## Rewards

At session end, Desmond gives each player a **unique pre-war item** from his collection:

| Item | Flavor |
|------|--------|
| Pre-war letter in a sealed envelope | Addressed to someone long dead — never opened |
| Working lighter, engraved name | *"To J.H. — Don't stop."* |
| Perfectly preserved bottle of bourbon | Single malt, 2069 vintage |
| Military commendation medal | Worn smooth from handling |
| Child's pre-war photograph | No one recognizes the family |

*No mechanical bonus unless the GM adds one. In a one-shot, flavor is everything.*

---

## Stats (Fallout 2d20)

Use **Ghoul (Feral Tendencies suppressed)** with high INT and AGI. Prefers ranged combat. Will retreat rather than die.
