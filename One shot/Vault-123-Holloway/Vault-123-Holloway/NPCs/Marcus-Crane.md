# 🧍 Marcus Crane

## Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Role** | Eliza's missing brother, the players' objective |
| **Condition** | Alive, drugged on swamp plant extract, cannot walk unaided |
| **Personality** | Scared, quiet, grateful — but complicated |

## Description

> *Pale from weeks of low light and poor food. His wrists are bound but not cut. The tribals kept him alive and mostly unharmed — which is somehow more unsettling than the alternative.*

---

## In Play

When rescued, Marcus is **too drugged to walk unassisted.** One player must physically support him throughout the exit sequence. This is intentional — it slows the pacing and adds weight to the rescue.

He says very little during the escape. He is processing.

**On the ferry back, quietly:**
> *"They sang to me every night. Not to scare me. Just... hymns. Old ones. From before."*

### Why This Line Matters

At face value: unsettling, humanizing, a grace note on Elias's character.

After the PROJECT HOLLOWAY reveal: this is the **last thing the scenario gave the players** before it ended. The extraction was complete. The simulation was winding down. Marcus delivered his final line.

Whether the scenario chose that line deliberately — whether Vault-Tec designed it to leave subjects with a particular emotional residue — is something your GM can decide.

**Do not resolve this moment on the night.** Let it sit. The pull-back follows immediately.

---

## After the Reveal

Marcus doesn't exist outside the Holloway scenario. He was a constructed character — part of the immersion architecture. His fear was engineered. His gratitude was engineered.

The hymns were real pre-war hymns, fed into the simulation from a Vault-Tec audio archive.

Whether that makes them less meaningful is a question worth leaving unanswered.
