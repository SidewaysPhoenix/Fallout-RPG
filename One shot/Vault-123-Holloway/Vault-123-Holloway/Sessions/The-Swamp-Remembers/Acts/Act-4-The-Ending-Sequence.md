# 🎬 Act 4 — The Glass World

## Summary

The moment of apparent victory dissolves into the reveal: the entire session was a **Personality Matrix Extraction** run as part of [[../../../Campaign/Vault-123|PROJECT HOLLOWAY]] inside Vault 123. The camera pulls back theatrically to show the players' characters in cryogenic stasis — their minds mapped, copied, and archived without their knowledge or consent.

---

## Trigger Point

Begin this sequence at the **peak of resolution** — the moment after Marcus is safe, caps are incoming, and everything feels like it might be okay. The dock. The salt air. The exhale.

---

## Read Aloud — The Transition

> *The salt air hits you. Marcus is safe. Caps are coming. For just a moment, everything in the wasteland feels like it might be okay.*
>
> *Then the fog rolls in. Not swamp fog — something cleaner. Colder. The dock lights flicker, and the rust on the railings looks... too perfect. Too symmetrical. Eliza reaches out to take her brother's hand and her fingers—*
>
> *—pass through him.*
>
> *The world stutters. Like a film reel catching. And then—*
>
> *White.*

---

## Read Aloud — The Pull-Back

*Shift tone here. Slow down. This is cinematic.*

> *Five figures. Each sealed in a curved pod of glass and steel, frost tracing the edges. Tubes. Monitors. The slow, even rhythm of preserved breath.*
>
> *On the wall above them, a painted mural — a blue and yellow crest, a smiling vault dweller, the words:*

**[Pause. Say this slowly.]**

> **VAULT 123 — YOUR MIND IS YOUR LEGACY.**

> *In the corner of the room, a single terminal blinks. Its last log entry reads:*
>
> *"PROJECT HOLLOWAY — Extraction Complete. Personality Matrix Integrity: 98.3%. Archived copies confirmed. Offsite backup pending. Awaiting Overseer authorization for matrix deployment."*
>
> *The lights dim to emergency amber. The hum continues.*
>
> *Somewhere above — miles above — the world is still burning.*
>
> *They don't know what was taken.*

---

## What PROJECT HOLLOWAY Means

The Point Lookout scenario was not a test. It was an **instrument** — a constructed psychological immersion designed to fully reveal each subject's personality so it could be mapped, recorded, and archived.

Every choice they made in the swamp. Every mercy shown to Elias. Every moment of fear or courage. All of it logged. All of it copied.

The characters in the pods are the **originals.** Their personalities exist somewhere else now too — in what form, your GM decides.

---

## GM Performance Notes

- **Do not rush this.** Silence between paragraphs lands harder than anything.
- Dim the lights or lower your voice to near-whisper for the pull-back.
- When players react with confusion or questions — say nothing. Just: *"The amber light hums. The stasis pods breathe."*
- If a player asks *"wait, was any of it real?"* — pause, then: *"The pods are real."*
- This is not a cliffhanger to resolve tonight. It is a **gift to the next GM.**
