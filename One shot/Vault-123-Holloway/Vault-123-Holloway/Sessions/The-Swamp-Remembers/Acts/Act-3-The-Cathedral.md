# ⛪ Act 3 — The Ark & Dove Cathedral

## Summary

The players assault or infiltrate the Cathedral, rescue Marcus, and confront [[../../NPCs/Brother-Elias|Brother Elias]] in a final encounter with multiple resolution paths.

---

## Approach Options

Let players choose how they enter. Each has different trade-offs.

| Approach | Method | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| **Front Gate** | Direct assault | Heavy guards, loud, higher combat load |
| **Flooded Crypt** | Desmond's intel | Stealth route, fewer enemies, more atmosphere |
| **Bluff Entry** | Pose as pilgrims | Deception TN 14 — success delays combat start |

> If players took the Tribal Scout intel, they get **+1d20 advantage** on the stealth approach.

---

## Room 1 — The Nave

**Read Aloud:**
> *Pews draped in swamp moss. Pre-war hymn books, soaked through and warped into something unrecognizable. A ghoul is chained to the altar at the far end — not aggressive, just... waiting. It watches you with hollow eyes.*

- **Enemies:** 2 Tribal Guards
- **Hazard:** Tripwire trap near the side aisle — **Perception TN 10** to spot
- The chained ghoul is non-hostile. Players can free it — it shuffles off into the swamp without a word.

---

## Room 2 — The Vestry

**No enemies.** This is the **empathy beat**.

- Contains [[../../Handouts/Elias-Journal|Brother Elias's Journal]]
- Key entry (read aloud or let players read the handout):

> *"They call me a monster. But I have seen what the old world left behind. I am trying to heal it. The land is sick. We are all sick. The sacrifice is not cruelty — it is medicine. It is the only medicine left."*

> This reframes Elias. He is not evil. He is **broken**. Let that sit before the final room.

---

## Room 3 — The Bell Tower / Ritual Chamber

See [[../../Encounters/Cathedral-Final-Encounter|Cathedral Final Encounter]] for full stat block and mechanics.

**Read Aloud (entering):**
> *Marcus is bound to a stone altar below, pale, breathing, alive. Above him, on a raised platform of rotting wood and salvaged steel, Brother Elias stands with arms spread wide. Three Tribal Zealots flank the walls.*
>
> *Elias does not attack. He looks at you with something that might be pity.*
>
> *"You came a long way," he says. "For what? To drag him back to a dying world?"*

### Elias's Monologue

Do not script this fully — improvise from his journal. Key points he believes:
- The wasteland is *diseased*, and the old world caused it
- Outsiders carry that disease
- The ritual is an act of love, not murder
- He genuinely grieves the necessity of it

---

## Resolution Paths

| Path | Requirement | Outcome |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Fight** | Defeat Elias and 3 Zealots | Marcus freed, Elias dead, cultists scatter |
| **Negotiate** | Speech TN 15 | Convince Elias Marcus is "unclean" (diseased, mutated, wrong blood) — he releases Marcus, party must leave Point Lookout permanently |
| **Expose the Lie** | Must have [[../../Handouts/Elias-Journal\|Elias's journal]] | Reveal to Zealots that Elias fabricated parts of their doctrine — Zealots stand down, Elias fights *alone* at reduced difficulty |

---

## After the Encounter

Marcus is alive but **drugged on swamp plant extract** — he cannot walk unaided and is confused. One player must physically support him on the way back.

He says very little. On the ferry back, quietly:
> *"They sang to me every night. Not to scare me. Just... hymns. Old ones. From before."*

**Leave that unresolved. The twist landing will recontextualize it.**
