# ⛪ Ark & Dove Cathedral

## Description

A pre-war Gothic church that survived the bombs through sheer mass of stone. The tribals have covered it in swamp moss, bone fetishes, and crude murals that blend pre-war religious iconography with wasteland ritual. Bells ring every dusk. The sound carries for miles.

**Atmosphere:**
> *The Cathedral rises out of the swamp like something that refused to die. Its stained glass is long shattered, the gaps covered with stretched hide. Bone chimes hang from the iron gate. Somewhere inside, someone is singing — low, slow, old words.*

| Method | Requirements | Advantage |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Front Gate | None | Straightforward — but heavily guarded |
| Flooded Crypt (east side) | Desmond's intel | Bypasses gate, stealth entry, fewer enemies |
| Bluff as Pilgrims | Deception TN 14 | Delays combat, gets inside before fighting starts |

> Tribal Scout intel: **+1d20 advantage** on Sneak tests if the scout was captured and interrogated.

---

## Room 1 — The Nave

**Enemies:** 2 Tribal Guards  
**Hazard:** Tripwire near side aisle — Perception TN 10 to spot

**Notable:** A ghoul is chained to the altar — not aggressive. It watches silently. Players can free it; it walks into the swamp without acknowledging them.

---

## Room 2 — The Vestry

**No enemies.** Empathy beat. Contains [[../../Handouts/Elias-Journal|Brother Elias's Journal]].

Key journal entry (read aloud or give handout):
> *"They call me a monster. But I have seen what the old world left behind. I am trying to heal it. The land is sick. We are all sick. The sacrifice is not cruelty — it is medicine. It is the only medicine left."*

---

## Room 3 — The Bell Tower / Ritual Chamber

Final encounter. See [[../../Encounters/Cathedral-Final-Encounter|Cathedral Final Encounter]].

Marcus is bound to the altar below. [[../../NPCs/Brother-Elias|Brother Elias]] stands on a raised platform above, flanked by 3 Tribal Zealots.

**Elias does not attack first.** He speaks. Let him.

---

## Post-Encounter

Once resolved, players escort Marcus out. He is drugged and cannot walk unaided. One player must support him.

The Cathedral can be looted, burned, or left standing — player choice. No mechanical consequence either way.
