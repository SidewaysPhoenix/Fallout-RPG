# 🌿 The Swamp Remembers — Overview

## Premise

A riverboat captain named **Tobar** has been ferrying desperate wastelanders to Point Lookout for caps — but several passengers never came back.

The players are hired by a **Rivet City merchant named Eliza Crane**, whose brother was on the last crossing. She wants answers and is offering **500 caps each** plus passage both ways.

## What They'll Uncover

The **Tribal cultists of the Ark & Dove Cathedral** have been capturing outsiders for a ritual sacrifice to their "great spirit." Eliza's brother **Marcus** is still alive — being prepared as the next offering.

## What They Won't Uncover (Until the End)

The entire scenario is a **Personality Matrix Extraction** — part of [[Vault-123|PROJECT HOLLOWAY]]. Every choice they make is being recorded. The swamp, the cultists, the rescue — all of it is a constructed psychological immersion fed directly into their unconscious minds while their bodies sleep in cryogenic stasis in Vault 123.

They were not tested. They were **opened up and copied.**

## Core Themes

- Survival vs. morality in the wasteland
- Inherited madness — the tribals didn't create the cult, they inherited it
- The violation of having something taken without your knowledge or consent

## Three Rewatch Moments

Plant these naturally. They only land after the reveal.

| Moment | Where | Meaning After Reveal |
|--------|-------|----------------------|
| Desmond: *"You've got that look. Like you're not sure if you're really here."* | His mansion | A simulation character saying something accidentally true |
| Elias's journal first entry: *"A woman in a white coat..."* | The Vestry | Dr. Holloway bleeding through the extraction barrier |
| Marcus on the ferry: *"They sang to me every night."* | Return crossing | The last line the scenario delivers before extraction ends |

## GM Tips

- **Lean into atmosphere.** Describe the mud, the rot, the distant bells constantly.
- **Desmond is your safety valve.** If players get stuck or near a TPK, he has reasons to intervene.
- **Don't over-prep Elias's speech.** Let it come from his journal entries naturally.
- **Plant the three rewatch moments without flagging them.** They only work if the players don't see them coming.
- For **2d20 pacing**, aim for 3 full combat encounters: river skirmish, factory, and cathedral. Everything else is skill tests and roleplay.
