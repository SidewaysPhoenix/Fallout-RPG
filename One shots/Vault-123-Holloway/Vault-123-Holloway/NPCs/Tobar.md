# ⚓ Tobar

## Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Role** | Ferryman between Rivet City and Point Lookout |
| **Vessel** | The Duchess Gambit |
| **Personality** | Greedy, cowardly, knows more than he says |
| **Motivation** | Caps. Just caps. He doesn't want trouble. |

## Description

> *A weathered man with the posture of someone who's been waiting to be punched for a long time and is surprised every day it hasn't happened yet. His eyes do the math on you the second you board.*

---

## What He Knows

- Several passengers have gone missing after arriving at Point Lookout
- He suspects (but won't confirm) the Tribals are responsible
- He has not reported this to anyone — it would cost him his route and his income

## Behavior in Play

**Before questioning:** Deflects, changes subject, talks about the weather and the river.

**On a successful Persuasion or Insight test (TN 12):**
> *"Look. Things go missing in Point Lookout. Always have. I'm just a ferryman. I get people there. What happens after ain't my business."*

**If cornered or threatened:**
He breaks easily and admits he saw two previous passengers being *followed* from the dock by Tribal scouts. He didn't intervene. He deeply regrets this and is ashamed of it — but shame doesn't make him brave.

---

## Resolution

- **Expose him to Eliza:** She's furious. He loses the contract, possibly gets run out of Rivet City.
- **Forgive him:** He's genuinely relieved. May offer future discounted passage.
- **Claim the Duchess Gambit:** If players expose him and he flees, the boat is effectively abandoned. Can be used as a fast-travel asset in future sessions.

---

## Stats (Fallout 2d20)

Use **Wastelander (Scavenger)** profile from the core book. He will not fight — he runs at the first sign of real violence.
