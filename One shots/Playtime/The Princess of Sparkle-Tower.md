# One-Shot Concept: **The Princess of Sparkle-Tower**

## Core idea

A tiny settlement called **Patchwork Village** has a beloved radio tower decorated with lights, flags, and old shiny hubcaps. The children of the settlement call it **Sparkle-Tower**, and the town uses it to broadcast bedtime stories, music, and safety messages.

One day, the tower goes silent.

The town’s “princess,” a clever young girl named **Princess Juniper**, went to the tower to fix the lights with her robot friend, **Sir Beeps-a-Lot**, but now she has not returned. The adults are worried, but the route to the tower is full of odd wasteland obstacles.

The player characters are asked to help reach Sparkle-Tower, find Juniper, and restore the “royal signal.”

The prince/princess angle is playful and metaphorical: Juniper is not actually royalty, but the kids in town made her a cardboard crown because she tells the best stories over the radio.

---

# Adventure Structure

## Scene 1: Patchwork Village

The party starts in a small, colorful settlement built around an old playground, diner, and bus stop.

The mayor, **Mayor Button**, explains:

“Princess Juniper went to Sparkle-Tower to fix the story-radio, but the signal went quiet! Without it, the little ones won’t get tonight’s bedtime broadcast.”

The party gets a simple royal quest:

**Find Princess Juniper, help Sir Beeps-a-Lot, and relight Sparkle-Tower.**

---

## Scene 2: The Junkwood Forest

On the way to the tower, the party enters a scrapyard overgrown with weeds. The kids in town call it **Junkwood Forest**.

Obstacle options:

### The Tin-Can Squirrels

A bunch of harmless mutated squirrels have stolen shiny parts from Sir Beeps-a-Lot. They are not evil; they just love shiny things.

Ways to solve:

- Sneak closer and retrieve the part
- Offer them something shinier
- Use Survival to understand their behavior
- Use Repair to make a substitute part

Suggested test:

- **CHA + Speech**, **PER + Survival**, or **AGI + Sneak**
- Difficulty 1 or 2

Failure should be funny, not punishing:

- the squirrels dump bottlecaps on someone’s head
- a character gets tangled in party streamers
- the group loses a few minutes

---

## Scene 3: The Bridge of the Grumpy Troll

A small footbridge over a dry drainage ditch is blocked by an old maintenance Protectron named **T.R.O.L.L.**

His full name is:

**Traffic Regulation and Obstacle Limitation Laborer**

He insists nobody may cross because “royal bridge tax has not been paid.”

He does not want money. He wants the correct answer to a riddle.

Possible riddles:

“What has keys but opens no locks?”

Answer: **a piano**

“What gets wetter the more it dries?”

Answer: **a towel**

“What has a face and hands but no arms or legs?”

Answer: **a clock**

Ways to solve:

- Answer the riddle
- Repair his old logic circuit
- Convince him the party is on royal business
- Show him a “quest badge” from Mayor Button

This is a great scene for the children to shine.

---

## Scene 4: The Castle That Was a Mall

Sparkle-Tower is on top of an old shopping mall. The children call it **The Glass Castle** because the broken storefronts glitter in the sun.

Inside are:

- old toy stores
- a food court
- a broken fountain
- mannequins dressed like knights
- a pre-war princess costume display
- a Nuka-Cola mascot statue

The party finds signs of Juniper:

- cardboard crown
- tool bag
- robot tracks
- a note: “Sir Beeps got scared. I’m going up!”

Obstacle: the escalator is broken.

Ways up:

- repair the escalator
- climb safely
- find the service stairs
- use a rope
- turn on emergency power

Suggested tests:

- **INT + Repair**
- **STR + Athletics**
- **PER + Survival**
- Difficulty 1 or 2

---

## Scene 5: The “Dragon” on the Roof

On the roof, the party hears a big roar.

It is not a real dragon.

It is an old pre-war parade machine shaped like a dragon, built for a children’s fantasy festival. Its speaker system is malfunctioning, making it roar and breathe harmless steam.

Princess Juniper is stuck in the radio control booth because the dragon machine is blocking the door. Sir Beeps-a-Lot is hiding behind an air conditioner, yelling:

“BRAVE KNIGHTS REQUIRED! VERY BRAVE! PREFERABLY WITH TOOLS!”

The dragon is the final encounter, but it should be solvable several ways.

Ways to defeat/help the dragon:

- Repair its control panel
- Calm Sir Beeps-a-Lot so he can help
- Climb around and unplug the steam hose
- Distract it with music
- Use Speech and pretend to be royal diplomats
- Use non-lethal attacks against its weak points

The twist:

The “dragon” was only trying to complete its old program:

**“Protect the Princess Parade until the Grand Finale.”**

The party can change its command to:

**“Help the Princess light the tower.”**

Then the dragon becomes an ally and uses its lights and speakers to restore the radio broadcast.

---

# Ending

The tower comes back on. Sparkle-Tower lights up. Patchwork Village hears Princess Juniper’s voice:

“Tonight’s story is about the brave knights who crossed Junkwood Forest, outwitted the bridge troll, entered the Glass Castle, and made friends with a dragon.”

The village celebrates with:

- Fancy Lad Snack Cakes
- Nuka-Cola floats
- paper crowns
- bottlecap medals

Rewards:

- **Medal of Sparkle-Tower**
- **Sir Beeps-a-Lot’s Thanks**
- 10–25 caps each
- a friendly robot contact
- the dragon machine occasionally roars happily during future broadcasts

# Main NPCs

## Princess Juniper

A smart, brave kid from Patchwork Village. She wears a cardboard crown and carries a tool belt.

Personality:

- kind
- curious
- slightly bossy in a funny way
- loves radio stories

## Sir Beeps-a-Lot

A Mr. Handy or Eyebot with a knightly personality.

Voice:  
“By my polished bolts, danger approaches!”

He is dramatic but cowardly.

## Mayor Button

A cheerful settlement leader who wears a coat covered in buttons.

## T.R.O.L.L.

A bridge-guard Protectron who loves rules and riddles.

## The Dragon

A malfunctioning parade robot. Scary for about ten seconds, then silly.

# Best Fallout 2d20 Adjustments for Kids

I would simplify the rules:

Use mostly **Difficulty 1 or 2** tests.

Let them describe what they want to do first, then pick the skill.

Use damage rarely. Instead of “you take damage,” use:

- you get startled
- you drop something
- you lose time
- your outfit gets covered in dust
- Sir Beeps panics loudly
- the squirrels steal another shiny thing

Use combat as slapstick:

- foam sword hits
- robots spin around
- steam clouds
- junk shields
- “bonk” instead of serious injury

Give frequent success:

- partial success still moves the story forward
- failure creates a funny complication
- reward creative ideas immediately
